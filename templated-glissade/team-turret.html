The Turret

This undertaking actually began as part of our final project for our intro to microelectronic circuits class, but it quickly turned into us having way too much fun with the thought of making a working, controllable turret.

After lots of work-hours and even more transistors (we found out our initial H-bridge wasn't providing enough power to the motors), we finished the turret!



Many thanks to PiE for their incredibly helpful advice, witty late-night conversations, and for letting us work all day (and all night) in 101!

One Final Note (Inspired by TF2)

Hey, look, buddy. We're engineers. That means we solve problems. Not problems like, "What is beauty?", because that would fall within the purview of your conundrums of philosophy. We solve practical problems.

For instance, how are we going to stop some obnoxious Mother Hubbard from invading our private space and interrupting our quiet time? The answer: use a (rubber band) gun. And if that don't work?

Use more rubber band gun.

Like this rubber-ammunition joystick-controlled little old number designed by us, built by us, and you best hope not pointed at you.
