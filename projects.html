Responsive Solar Panel Arrays

Responsive solar panels differ from traditional solar panels in that they respond to their environment. Their ability to adjust allows them greater efficiency than their nonresponsive counterparts; however, the equipment necessary to create such panels often requires extensive training, and the cost of access to the facilities that own them frequently prevents all but those with corporate backing or research stipends from using them. Such panels are also not mass-produced to the extent that nonresponsive panels are, and this in turn limits their effect on the solar energy industry.

Furthermore, current methods of mechanical responsiveness rely on sensors, motors, and other electronic devices which consume power and decrease the net output of the solar panels. Our objective is to create a means of generating a mechanical response that does not rely on electronic devices

I'm currently working with Joanne Lo on finding a means of facilitating such a response that is both simple and low cost.
